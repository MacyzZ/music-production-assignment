Three displays

Why?

Sound is a longitudinal wave, therefore it is very hard to visualize it or display it in that format...

It is necessary to see frequencies,these frequencies help to understand what we hear...

So to get a good visual of sound,we use three different displays what are listed below

Three displays

  • Oscilloscope display
  • Spectrum analyzer
  • Spectrogram

Oscilloscope display

Display the wave form

Amplitude vertically

Time horizontally

The wave form is actually the exact path that the speaker's going to make when sound is eventually made in the air by the speaker.

Defect

Frequency is necessary,and oscilloscope display doesn't provide a good idea of the frequency or timbre of the moment.

Spectrum analyzer

Display which frequencies the sound has lot of energy at

Amplitude vertically

Frequency horizontally

Defect

No sense of where things change over time

Spectrogram

Provide full picture of sound

  • Like spectrum display but it repeats over time
  • Provides a sense of how the timbre, the frequency, the amplitude, all change over time
  • Most represents the way we really hear
